Dear Hiring Team:

I am applying for the AI Transformation and Learning Associate Director role because it sits very close to the work I have been doing, and building toward, over the past several years: helping legal teams move from interest in new technology to confident, responsible use in daily practice.

From inside a law firm, I have helped support an enterprise AI pilot from the ground level. That work has included identifying practical use cases, helping design role-based training for staff, paralegals, attorneys, and partners, building workflow guides, and thinking through the responsible-AI standards that matter most in a legal environment: confidentiality, source verification, human review, and clear limits on what the technology should and should not do. I have also helped gather feedback from the people actually using the tools, which is often where the most important adoption lessons appear.

What I would bring that is less common is a formal learning and communication background. My graduate training in biomedical visualization, scientific communication, and learning theory has focused on how people understand complex information, especially when the stakes are high and the audience brings different levels of background knowledge. That has shaped how I think about AI enablement. A law firm is not one audience. Partners, associates, paralegals, case managers, administrative staff, and clients all come to these tools with different needs, concerns, and levels of technical confidence. Effective training has to meet people inside the work they actually do.

That is why I am especially interested in Sidley’s focus on instructional design, change management, responsible-AI standards, and AI Champions. I think successful adoption requires more than broad training or a polished demonstration. It requires role-specific examples, guided practice, office hours, searchable resources, clear guardrails, and a feedback loop that helps the program improve as users learn what works.

I am a daily user of the platforms this work runs on, including Claude, ChatGPT, Microsoft 365 Copilot, Gemini, and NotebookLM. I am comfortable with prompt design, structured workflows, retrieval and source-based AI concepts, and the practical work of turning an AI capability into something a busy legal team can use. I also bring full-stack coding foundations and experience thinking about how adoption and impact should be measured, including time saved, quality, confidence, and repeat use.

I am also comfortable presenting and teaching. Much of my work, both professionally and academically, has involved translating technical material for mixed audiences. I enjoy helping people move from uncertainty to practical confidence, especially when the goal is not simply to understand a tool but to change the way work moves through a firm.

For this application, I have built a portfolio that includes an interactive AI-enablement module, the instructional-design thinking behind it, and an adoption-and-value tracker. The materials are intended to show how I approach this work: begin with a real workflow, teach each role differently, put the guardrails first, measure adoption and confidence, and make the answers easy to find again.

I would welcome the opportunity to bring my experience in litigation operations, instructional design, scientific communication, and responsible-AI enablement to Sidley’s AI transformation and learning program. Thank you for your consideration.
